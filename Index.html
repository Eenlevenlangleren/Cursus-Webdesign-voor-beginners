Alaa Salah, a Sudanese woman propelled to internet fame after clips went viral of her leading powerful protest chants against President Omar al-Bashir, addresses protestors during a demonstration in front of the militairy headquarters in the capitol Khartoum on April 10,2019. Dubbed on-line as Kandaka, "Nubian queen" she has become a symbol of the protests which she says have traditionally had a female backbone in Sudan. When Hindi Makki, A Sudanese American interfaith and racisme educator in Chicago, first saw the image, she was blown away. "I" was rendered speechless", she said, because of the amazing imagery and the symbolism that was inherent in the photo. The image was taken by Sudanese photographer Lana Haroun in the capital of Khartoum.
